5. News Source Attribution

In Short

Earth00 aggregates news from publicly available RSS feeds and web pages published by hundreds of news organizations worldwide. We do not claim authorship of, or ownership in, source headlines, article text, or images.

How attribution works

For each article surfaced in the application:

  • The publisher's name (or hostname) is shown alongside the headline
  • The original article URL is reachable via tap, click, or "open source"
  • Excerpts are limited to a brief snippet sufficient to convey the topic
  • We do not host full article bodies, paywalled content, or images embedded from third-party CDNs without the publisher's implicit permission via their RSS feed

Aggregation framework

Earth00 operates under the framework of fair use (17 U.S.C. § 107) for the limited use of headlines and brief snippets, with prominent attribution and links back to original sources. We respect robots.txt directives and rate-limit requests to publisher servers.

6. AI Content Disclosure

In Short

Some features in Earth00 produce content using third-party large language models (primarily Anthropic's Claude). These features include — but are not limited to — daily briefings, story analyses, contextual explanations, and translations.

What this means for you

  • Outputs are AI-assisted and may contain inaccuracies, biases, or hallucinations
  • You should not rely on AI-generated content as the sole source of truth, particularly for important decisions
  • The original article linked beneath each AI-generated summary is the authoritative source
  • We do not train AI models on user data; LLM providers process data per their own privacy policies

Where reasonably possible, AI-generated content is labeled within the application interface.

7. Content Removal Policy

In Short

Earth00 supports the takedown framework set out in the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 512) and analogous frameworks in other jurisdictions.

Who can request removal

Any of the following parties may submit a removal request:

  • A news publisher (or its authorized agent) requesting that their content no longer be aggregated by Earth00
  • A copyright holder (or authorized agent) of any specific work appearing in the Services
  • A subject of news coverage with a legitimate privacy or right-of-publicity claim
  • Any individual whose personal data appears in the Services without lawful basis

How to submit a removal request

Send a written notice to legal@earth00.com containing:

  • Your full name and contact information (mailing address, telephone, email)
  • If acting on behalf of a publisher or rights holder, identification of that party and your authority to act
  • A description of the work or content for which removal is requested
  • The URL(s), publisher name(s), or other identifiers sufficient for us to locate the material
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the rights holder, its agent, or the law
  • A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the rights holder
  • A physical or electronic signature

What we will do

  • Acknowledge receipt within five (5) business days
  • Review and act on valid requests within fourteen (14) days of receipt
  • For requests from publishers: cease aggregating the publisher's RSS feed and web pages, and remove existing snippets/headlines from active surfaces
  • For requests targeting specific content: remove the identified URL(s), snippets, and any AI-generated summaries derived therefrom
  • Notify you of the action taken, by reply to your original request

8. Counter-Notification

In Short

If you believe content was removed in error or as a result of misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification to legal@earth00.com.

A valid counter-notification must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the material removed and its location prior to removal
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good faith belief the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification
  • Your name, address, and telephone number, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal courts of the State of Delaware
  • A statement that you will accept service of process from the original complaining party
